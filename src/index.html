Tim "Avicii" Bergling

1989-2018

Tim Bergling, known professionally as Avicii was a Swedish electronic musician, DJ, and songwriter who specialized in audio programming, remixing and record producing.At the age of 16, Bergling began posting his remixes on electronic music forums, which led to his first record deal.

Tim Bergling, has moved countless fans across the world with his exceptional music. When Avicii was tragically found dead on Apr. 20, 2018 at the age of just 28, the world was devestated. His apparent suicide is a reminder of the immense pressures that the music indusrty in particuar often plases upon performers, and the lackluster understanding of musicians, and all people's, vitally important mental health needs. Though Avicii's life was heartbreakingly short, the power of the music he created will continue to affect the lives of many for ages to come, for in the words of Bono, “Music can change the world, because it can change people.”

A BRIEF TIMELINE(1989-2018)

1989

September 8, 1989 – Born as Tim Bergling in Stockholm, Sweden

2007

At the age of 16, he began making music. In May 2007, Avicii signed on with the Dejfitts Plays label

2009

By 2009 to 2010, Bergling was already a prolific producer and was releasing music incredibly quickly, including such tracks as "Sound of Now", "Muja", and "Ryu".

2010

In 2010 Bergling released the hit song "Seek Bromance", which reached the top 20 in several countries.

2011

“Levels” was released, which launched him into the mainstream and superstardom.

2012

In 2012, his collaboration track "Sunshine" with David Guetta was nominated for a Grammy award under the category for Best Dance Recording.

2013

True era unfolded, including “Wake Me Up” and “Hey Brother”

2014

Bergling announced he had been hospitalized. Stayed busy in studio, working on 70 songs for his next album Stories

2015

Avicii's next album, Stories, was released on 2 October 2015 alongside 3 promotional singles: "Broken Arrows" with Zac Brown, "Ten More Days" with Zak Abel and "Gonna Love Ya" with Sandro Cavazza.

2016

On 29 March 2016, Bergling announced that he would be retiring from performing live and touring for the remainder of 2016, indefinitely.

2017

On September 11, 2017, The documentary Avicii:True Stories chronicles the artist's retirement from touring and features interviews from his colleagues David Guetta, Tiësto, Wyclef Jean, Nile Rodgers and Chris Martin of Coldplay

2018

April 20, 2018 – Avicii died at the age of 28 in Muscat, Oman.
